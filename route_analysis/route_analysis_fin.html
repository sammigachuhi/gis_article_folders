Introduction

Now it so happens that we want to calculated the distance between two points. That is, the shortest route distance. This form of distance calculation is the simplest form of route analysis. Route analysis is part of the larger network form of analyses. Within route analysis, one can engage to find out the shortest, fastest or even the most scenic route. It all depends on the impedance you are trying to overcome.

Today we shall endeavour to calculate a route analysis of the shortest distance between two points. In a previous exercise we took a long stroll along the beach by working with two famous tourist points. One of them was the Vasco da Gama Pillar in Malindi. Today we shall work with a different point, Malindi International Airport. Therefore, we shall endeavour to find out the shortest distance between the two points of interest.

As always, give a geographer a point on the earth's surface and he shall make his way around. Here are the coordinates for our two chosen places for this exercise.

Now, we would like to calculate the shortest route distance between the two above points and extract the route. Our source of the route, say the road, will be from an Open Streetmap (OSM) network. The osmnx package is the tool which enables us to extract network components, such as edges, nodes and polygons from our OSM. Now if you are new to GIS, the term edges and nodes may confuse you. But don't let it scare you, you can think of edges as any line that connects between two points. Unlike in maths where you think it as the line between two triangle corners, in GIS it is more like a road connecting from point A to B. Nodes are simply the points that mark the beginning or ending points of a line, which we have called here an edge. Example, from junction A to junction B. A picture is worth a thousand words, so here you go...

In [107]:
# Create function to convert row column values to km
def convert_m_km(geodataframe, column_name):
    return geodataframe.assign(length_km= lambda x: x.length_m / 1000)

The purpose of the above function is as follows. The function convert_m_km takes in a geodataframe (geodataframe) and one specific column (column_name) and calculates the units in column_name into metres using the formula:

km = row_column / 1000

We have just minimized what would have been two lines into one using the lambda function.
